How Waypoint Labs Could Be Used in Mining Operations in Australia

This document maps existing Waypoint Labs features to mining use cases in Australia and suggests integration patterns.

1. Surveying & Volumetric Measurement

  • Use: General and custom route planning for systematic pit, stockpile, dump and rehab surveys; VolumetricTool and PhotogrammetryPanel for volume and terrain.
  • Australia: Regular pit surveys, stockpile volumes (ROM, product, overburden), tailings dam surveys, rehabilitation progress. Fly repeat grids; export to mine planning / GIS.
  • Integration: Point SDK waypoints at your survey grid; feed volumes into mine software (e.g. Deswik, Vulcan, Surpac) or GIS.

2. Infrastructure & Equipment Inspection

  • Use: InspectionWorkflow, MissionTemplates, ConstructionProgressTracker-style repeat missions; ManualControlPanel and CesiumMissionView for close-up and 3D context.
  • Australia: Conveyors, crushers, stackers, reclaimers, highwalls, haul roads, berms, drainage, camp and workshop buildings.
  • Integration: Define inspection waypoints once; run via SDK command/waypoints on a schedule; send images/video to asset or CMMS systems.

3. Safety, Exclusion Zones & People/Vehicle Awareness

  • Use: People recognition and animal/person detection; AirspaceChecker, ComplianceDashboard, FailsafeManager for rules and geofencing.
  • Australia: Blast exclusion zones, haul road crossings, PPE/vehicle-in-pit checks, “man down” or vehicle-in-danger awareness near equipment.
  • Integration: Use detection API and WebSocket for real-time alerts; tie route planning to “no-fly” or “caution” zones that align with CASA and site safety rules.

4. Environmental & Rehabilitation (Australian Focus)

  • Use: ConservationMode (population/health), AnimalDetector, PrecisionAgriculture-style analysis (vegetation/health), AnalyticsDashboard for trends.
  • Australia: Fauna monitoring (e.g. before/after blasting, near rehab), erosion and vegetation cover, water and sediment, compliance with environmental conditions and closure criteria.
  • Integration: Run general route planning over set transects or rehab areas; feed detections and simple indices into environmental reporting tools.

5. Dust, Emissions & Visual Monitoring

  • Use: Multi-drone video and live feed for visual monitoring; AnalyticsDashboard for time-based views; optional custom analytics on camera feeds.
  • Australia: Dust from haul roads, crushers, stockpiles; visible emissions; community and regulator visibility.
  • Integration: Stream from MultiDroneVideoManager to recording or monitoring platforms; use AI chat for “where is dust worst today?” once you add simple dust/visibility logic.

6. Remote & Offline (Regional Australia)

  • Use: OfflineMissionManager, MissionTemplates, autonomous route execution; SDK command/waypoints for pre-planned missions.
  • Australia: Poor or no connectivity at pit, exploration, or rehab sites; FIFO rosters with pre-loaded missions.
  • Integration: Plan missions in office or camp; sync to tablet/laptop; run missions offline and upload data when back in range.

7. Regulatory (CASA, Remote ID, Airspace)

  • Use: AirspaceChecker, RemoteIDManager, ComplianceDashboard, FailsafeManager.
  • Australia: CASA Part 101 (and future rules), Remote ID, controlled airspace near ports/airfields, mine airspace coordination.
  • Integration: Extend AirspaceChecker with Australian airspace data (e.g. CASA/airservices); use RemoteIDManager and compliance views for audits and approvals.

8. Incident Response & Emergency

  • Use: SAR-style use of people detection, general route planning, AI chat for “quickest safe route to X”, multi-drone video for command centre.
  • Australia: Search after blast, vehicle rollover, medical emergency in pit, bushfire or flood impact on site.
  • Integration: One-click “incident route” templates; stream from MultiDroneVideoManager to site emergency or control room.

Practical Next Steps for Australian Mining

  1. Add a “Mining” mode to the Civilian app that defaults to survey grids, inspection templates, and Australian airspace/compliance.
  2. Wire SDK commands to your preferred drone stack (MAVLink/DroneKit or vendor SDK) and test in a controlled area.
  3. Plug Australian airspace (and optionally mine-specific geofences) into AirspaceChecker and surface compliance in ComplianceDashboard.
  4. Reuse Python modules (route planner, detector, analytics) in mine-specific scripts or dashboards for volumes, inspections, and environmental reporting.
